Sri Lankan Rice Cake

20m prep | 1hr cook | 4 servings

One of the most fabulous and delicious recipes

Description:

Health Score:Unknown

Calories per serve: Uknown

Allergens: Uknown

Rice Cake (Kiribath) is a classic Sri Lankan dish shaped as a cake, made of rice and coconut milk. It is a celebratory treat for all special occasions and is also a traditional Sri Lankan breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb white short grain rice (ideally Sri Lankan rice called kekulu)
  • 3 cups thick coconut milk
  • 5 cups water
  • Salt to taste

Steps:

    Step 1

  1. Wash the rice and place in a large saucepan with the water and salt.

    2. Step 2

  2. Bring to a boil uncovered, over medium heat.

    3. Step 3

  3. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer on medium / low heat until water is absorbed and rice is tender (about 20 to 25 minutes).

    4. Step 4

  4. Add the coconut milk and stir well until everything is mixed.

    5. Step 5

  5. Simmer on low heat until the rice absorbs all the coconut milk (about 15 minutes).

    6. Step 6

  6. Remove from fire and let cool for 5 minutes.

    7. Step 7

  7. Transfer the mixture to a large shallow dish and flatten with the back of a flat spoon, spatula or parchment paper.

    8. Step 8

  8. Draw lines on the top surface in the shape of diamond or square.

    9. Step 9

  9. Let cool and solidify for about 10 minutes before cutting into pieces.

    10. Step 10

  10. Serve with seeni sambol and / or lunu miris.
